## What is Terminal?

**Terminal** is a program on your Mac that lets you type commands to control your computer. It's like a text-based way to talk to your computer.

## How to Open Terminal

### Method 1: Using Spotlight Search (Easiest)

1. Press `Command + Space` (hold both keys at the same time)
2. Type: `Terminal`
3. Press `Enter`
4. A black or white window will open - that's Terminal!

### Method 2: Using Finder

1. Open **Finder** (the blue face icon in your dock)
2. Click **"Applications"** in the sidebar
3. Open the **"Utilities"** folder
4. Double-click **"Terminal"**

### Method 3: Using Launchpad

1. Press `F4` or swipe up with 4 fingers on trackpad
2. Type: `Terminal`
3. Click on the Terminal icon

---

## What Terminal Looks Like

When you open Terminal, you'll see:
- A window (usually black background with white text, or white with black text)
- A prompt that looks like: `yourname@yourcomputer ~ %`
- A blinking cursor where you can type

---

## Opening Multiple Terminal Windows

You need **2 Terminal windows** open at the same time:

### To Open a Second Terminal Window:

**Option 1: New Tab**
- In Terminal, press `Command + T`
- This opens a new tab (you'll see tabs at the top)

**Option 2: New Window**
- In Terminal, click **"Shell"** in the menu bar
- Click **"New Window"**
- This opens a completely new Terminal window

**Option 3: Just Open Terminal Again**
- Press `Command + Space`
- Type `Terminal`
- Press `Enter`
- This opens another Terminal window
